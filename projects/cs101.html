ABCs of CS101

LTP project proposal designed for the introductory computer science class (CS101) at Duke University

Developed by Regan Baum

Goals

Students will collaboratively create a new study tool for CS101.

Students will think deeply about their experiences with specific topics in CS101.

Students will gain a better understanding of the big picture ideas of CS101.

Details

Location

CS101 classrooms and surrounding Duke campus

Participants

CS101 students at Duke

Duration

Two lab sessions (approx. 2.5 hrs)

Materials

Phones/Cameras

Printer

Construction Paper

Scissors

Glue

Markers

Steps

Lab Session 1

  1. Create groups of about 3-5 students per group.
  2. Give each group some letters from the alphabet. Make sure that each letter is represented in at least one group.
  3. Give students 5-10 minutes to brainstorm words that represent computer science topics or concepts for their letters. For the more challenging letters, encourage students to think about the names of assignments or problems they have done as well.
  4. Ask each group to narrow down one word per letter for each of their letters.
  5. Give students about 10 minutes to brainstorm how they would like to photograph each word.
  6. The rest of class time will be used for taking photos.
  7. Students should send their photos to their lab instructor before the next lab session.

Lab Session 2

  1. Before this lab session, lab instructors should print out all photos that were taken.
  2. Return the printed photos to their respective groups.
  3. Have students glue each photo to one piece of construction paper and then write or draw something to go along with the photo. This could be a code snippet, a visualization of some sort, a story, or anything else that relates to the photo.
  4. Leave at least 15 minutes at the end to put the alphabet in order and display it on the walls of the classroom. Let students walk around and look at the completed alphabet.
  5. After the lab session is over, scan each letter to save it electronically. Give students access to all the alphabets created from every lab session to use as a study tool.

Inspiration and Rationale

The idea for this project came about when Regan was a Teaching Associate (TA) for the introductory computer science course at Duke University. Currently, there are not many personal connections that can be made between students and the computer science curriculum. Additionally, students sometimes feel discouraged when they do not understand concepts. The goal of implementing this LTP project in an introductory computer science class is that students will feel more connected to the material as well as more confident in their technical abilities.

Regan enjoys being a TA for the introductory computer science course. She would love to work with this community on this project because she wants to help students feel more confident in their abilities to learn fundamental computer science concepts. As a software engineer, this theme appeals to Regan because she uses core concepts of computer science almost everyday. The ABCs of CS101 project will help students in the introductory class solidify their knowledge of these concepts, hopefully encouraging them to pursue other computer science courses as well.

As with any project, some challenges may arise when carrying it out. For this project, time may present itself to be a challenge. There is often a lot of material to get through, and the professors might not be able to devote enough time to implement this LTP project. If time is a concern, the project could be an extra credit opportunity for students to encourage them to participate. Additionally, this project may be logistically challenging in terms of collecting all the photos and distributing them to the appropriate lab section; however, since the introductory computer science course at Duke has a large teaching staff and lots of TAs, tasks can be divided up accordingly to ensure that photos end up in the correct labs.
